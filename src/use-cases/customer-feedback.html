Customer feedback

Product teams collect feedback across email, chat, and support tickets — with no easy way to see patterns. Here's how a team turns it into one shared, searchable table just by describing it.

  • Built by chatting
  • Logged in plain language
  • See the trend at a glance

01 The problem

The feedback is everywhere but here

A great idea lands in email. A bug report shows up in Slack. Support closes a ticket with a gem buried inside. The team knows the signal is there, but it's spread across five places and nobody can see the pattern.

You don't want another tool to manage. You want every note in one list.

02 Just describe it

Say it in a sentence

You type what a piece of feedback looks like in plain English. TypeTable suggests a table — which fields to include and what rules to apply — and waits for your OK.

The rating has to be 1 to 5, so a typo can never turn into a wild score, and new notes start as Open on their own.
