For Sales

Deal pipeline

Sales teams keep deals in a spreadsheet that never matches the forecast. Here's how a team rebuilds the whole pipeline in TypeTable — just by describing it, in an afternoon.

  • Built by chatting
  • Team updates in plain language
  • Locked for the forecast

01 The problem

The forecast is a guess

Every rep keeps deals in their own tab. Stages are spelled five different ways, values are stale, and by Friday nobody trusts the number. Someone rebuilds the sheet by hand before every review.

Nobody wants a CRM project. They just want the list to be right.

Sales ops The problem today
You

Our deals are scattered across everyone's spreadsheets. The forecast never matches reality.

TypeTable

Let's give the team one shared pipeline. Tell me what you track on a deal and I'll build it.

02 Just describe it

Say it in a sentence

You type what a deal looks like in plain English. TypeTable proposes the table — the fields, the types, and the rules — and waits for you to approve.

The stage becomes a fixed list, so "in progress" and "almost closed" can never sneak in again.

Sales ops Creates the pipeline
You

Track account, stage, value, owner, expected close date, and the next step.

TypeTable Deal pipeline

Here's the table I'd create:

  • Account
  • Stage
  • Value
  • Owner
  • Close date
  • Next step

Stage must be one of Lead · Qualified · Proposal · Won · Lost. Value in USD.
