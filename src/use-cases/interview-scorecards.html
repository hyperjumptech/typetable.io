For Hiring

Interview notes

Interview notes arrive in every format — Slack, docs, memory — and never line up. Here's how a hiring team gives every interviewer the same simple scorecard just by describing it.

  • Built by chatting
  • One scorecard per interview
  • Easy to compare

01 The problem

Notes live everywhere

One interviewer drops a thumbs-up in Slack. Another writes half a page in a doc. A third just remembers. When it's time to decide, you can't line the candidates up side by side.

The team doesn't want a hiring platform. It wants every interview written down the same way.

Hiring manager Describes the problem
You

Our interview notes are scattered across Slack, docs, and people's memory. I can't compare candidates fairly.

TypeTable

Let's give everyone one scorecard to fill in. Tell me what you want to capture after an interview and I'll build it.

02 Just describe it

Say it in a sentence

You type what an interview scorecard should hold in plain English. TypeTable proposes the table — the fields, the types, and the rules — and waits for you to approve.

The recommendation becomes a fixed list, so everyone answers on the same scale.
